Ada Lovelace: The Pioneer of Computer Programming

A portrait of Ada Lovelace, known for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Ada Lovelace, born in 1815, was a British mathematician and writer. She is recognized as the first computer programmer due to her work on the Analytical Engine, an early mechanical computer. Lovelace's notes on the machine include what is recognized as the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine. She is considered by many as a visionary who predicted the ability of computers to go beyond mere number-crunching.
